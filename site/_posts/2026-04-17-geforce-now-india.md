## What's Happening
NVIDIA's GeForce Now has been making waves in the gaming industry, and its launch in India is a significant milestone. The service allows gamers to play high-quality games on any device with an internet connection, eliminating the need for expensive hardware upgrades. With GeForce Now, gamers can access a vast library of games, including popular titles like Fortnite, PUBG, and Assassin's Creed, without the need for lengthy downloads or installations. The service uses NVIDIA's advanced graphics processing units (GPUs) to render games in the cloud, providing a seamless and lag-free gaming experience.

The launch of GeForce Now in India is a strategic move by NVIDIA, as the country is home to a large and growing gaming community. According to a report by KPMG, the Indian gaming market is expected to reach $1.1 billion by 2025, with cloud gaming being a key driver of growth. With GeForce Now, NVIDIA is well-positioned to capitalize on this trend and establish itself as a leader in the Indian gaming market. The service is available in two pricing tiers: a free tier with limited gameplay time and a paid tier with unlimited gameplay time and priority access to servers.

The pricing of GeForce Now is a major factor in its appeal, with the paid tier costing just ₹499 per month. This is significantly cheaper than other cloud gaming services, such as Google Stadia and Microsoft xCloud, which cost ₹699 and ₹799 per month, respectively. The affordability of GeForce Now makes it an attractive option for gamers in India, who can now access high-quality games without breaking the bank. With its competitive pricing and impressive performance, GeForce Now is set to disrupt the Indian gaming market and change the way gamers play games.

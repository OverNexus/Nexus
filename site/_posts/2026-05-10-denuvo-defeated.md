## What's Happening
The demise of Denuvo as an effective DRM tool has been a long time coming. First introduced in 2016, Denuvo was touted as the ultimate solution to PC gaming piracy. It used a complex system of encryption and obfuscation to protect games from being cracked. However, the cracking community, led by groups such as CPY and CODEX, has consistently found ways to bypass Denuvo's protections. Over the years, Denuvo has been defeated on numerous occasions, with many high-profile games being cracked within days of their release. The most recent example is the cracking of 2K's latest game, which was protected by Denuvo. This has led 2K to abandon Denuvo in favor of an online check-in requirement, which requires players to periodically connect to the internet to verify their game ownership.
The online check-in requirement is seen as a more restrictive form of DRM, as it requires players to have a constant internet connection to play the game. This has been met with criticism from gamers, who see it as an annoyance and an invasion of their privacy. The move is also seen as a sign of desperation from 2K, which is struggling to find an effective way to protect its games from piracy.

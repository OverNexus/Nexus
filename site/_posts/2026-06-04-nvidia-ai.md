## What's Happening
Two years have passed since Nvidia introduced Copilot+, a technology that aimed to bring AI-powered assistance to PCs. Now, the company is taking another significant step in this direction, with Jensen Huang, Nvidia's CEO, stating that humans essentially 'rent' PC cores and that AI agents are more deserving of that processing power. This statement has sparked a lot of debate and discussion within the tech community, with many wondering what this means for the future of PCs and the role of AI in our daily lives. Huang's vision is to create a world where AI is seamlessly integrated into our devices, making them more powerful, efficient, and capable of performing complex tasks.

The introduction of Copilot+ was a significant milestone for Nvidia, as it marked the company's first major foray into the world of AI-powered PCs. The technology was designed to provide users with a range of AI-driven tools and features, from basic tasks like data analysis to more complex applications like gaming and content creation. With the latest announcement, it's clear that Nvidia is committed to continuing down this path, with a focus on developing more advanced AI capabilities and integrating them deeper into PC architecture.

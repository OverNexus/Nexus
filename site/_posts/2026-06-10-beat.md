## TL;DR
The enduring appeal of '90s-inspired beat 'em ups has been a topic of discussion among gamers, with Marvel Games weighing in on the phenomenon. Beat 'em ups, a staple of '90s gaming, continue to influence modern games. With their unique blend of action and combat, these games have stood the test of time. ## What's Happening
The '90s was a pivotal time for the gaming industry, with the rise of iconic beat 'em ups like Street Fighter, Mortal Kombat, and Double Dragon. These games not only defined the genre but also paved the way for future fighting games. Marvel Games, in a recent interview with IGN, discussed the lasting appeal of '90s-inspired beat 'em ups, citing their simplicity, accessibility, and addictive gameplay as key factors. According to Bill Rosemann, Executive Creative Director at Marvel Games, the '90s beat 'em ups were more than just games - they were a cultural phenomenon. The interview highlighted the importance of understanding the roots of the genre and how it has evolved over time.

The '90s beat 'em ups were known for their over-the-top action, colorful characters, and competitive multiplayer. Games like X-Men: Mutant Apocalypse and The Punisher showcased the potential of the genre, with their fast-paced combat and engaging storylines. The success of these games led to a proliferation of beat 'em ups in the '90s, with numerous titles being released across various platforms. The genre's popularity peaked in the mid-to-late '90s, with the release of games like Final Fight and Streets of Rage. Although the genre's popularity waned in the early 2000s, its influence can still be seen in modern games.

The recent resurgence of interest in '90s-inspired beat 'em ups can be attributed to the nostalgia factor, as well as the rise of indie game development. Games like Streets of Rage 4 and Battletoads have successfully revived the genre, introducing it to a new generation of gamers. The success of these games has also led to the development of new beat 'em ups, such as Fight'N Rage and The TakeOver. Marvel Games' own foray into the genre, with games like Marvel's Avengers and Marvel's Spider-Man, has also contributed to the resurgence of interest in beat 'em ups.

## Deep Dive
From a technical standpoint, '90s beat 'em ups were characterized by their side-scrolling gameplay, simple controls, and emphasis on combat. The games' visuals and sound design were also notable, with the use of vibrant colors, detailed character sprites, and catchy soundtracks. The genre's simplicity and accessibility made it appealing to a wide audience, with games like Double Dragon and Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles: Turtles in Time becoming staples of '90s gaming. In terms of market data, the beat 'em up genre was a significant contributor to the gaming industry's revenue in the '90s, with games like Street Fighter II and Mortal Kombat generating millions of dollars in sales.

The modern beat 'em up scene has evolved significantly, with the incorporation of new features like 3D graphics, online multiplayer, and complex combat mechanics. Games like Devil May Cry and Bayonetta have pushed the boundaries of the genre, introducing new gameplay mechanics and raising the bar for visuals and sound design. The rise of indie game development has also led to the creation of new beat 'em ups, such as Cuphead and Dead Cells, which have garnered critical acclaim and commercial success. According to a report by the Entertainment Software Association, the fighting game genre, which includes beat 'em ups, generated over $1 billion in revenue in 2020.

The influence of '90s beat 'em ups can also be seen in other genres, such as action-adventure games and role-playing games. Games like God of War and Diablo have incorporated elements of beat 'em ups, such as combat mechanics and boss battles, into their gameplay. The use of nostalgia as a marketing tool has also become a common practice in the gaming industry, with many games incorporating retro elements and gameplay mechanics into their design. For example, the recent release of Streets of Rage 4 included a retro mode, which allowed players to experience the game with classic graphics and sound design.
